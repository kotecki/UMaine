About me:
Dr. Kotecki joined the Electrical and Computer Engineering Department at the University of Maine in September 1999. His research focus is in the areas of Microelectronics, Analog and Mixed-Signal Circuit Design, Electronic Materials, and Computer Modeling and Simulation. Prior to joining the faculty at UMaine, he was a senior engineer at the IBM Microelectronics Division, Semiconductor Research and Development Center in Hopewell Junction, NY. He received a Ph.D. in Engineering Applied Science from the University of California, Davis in 1988 and performed research on laser assisted chemical vapor deposition at the Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory in California. He has received 60 U.S. Patents for inventions related to semiconductor processing, microelectronic structures and devices and has authored or co-authored more than 70 papers in these areas. He is a senior member of IEEE and a member of APS and Tau Beta Pi. Dr. Kotecki is a licensed Patent Agent and a consultant at Blue Hill Innovation LLC.
